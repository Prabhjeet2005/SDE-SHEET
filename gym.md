## 🍳 Protein Strategy: How to Fix Your Diet
Because you have more access to eggs, they will be your primary muscle builder. Normal Indian meals are high in carbohydrates and low in protein. You do not need to ask your mom to cook separate meals; you simply need to add a structural protein foundation around what she already makes. [2, 3] 
## Your Daily Protein Architecture (Aiming for ~140g–150g)

* Morning / Breakfast: Eat 4 whole boiled eggs alongside whatever breakfast or stuffed parantha is made. [2, 4] 
* Lunch: Drink a large glass of your mom's lassi / dahi, but eat only 2 rotis instead of 3 or 4. ## 🚶‍♂️ How to Integrate Your Treadmill Walkpad
Do not run on the walkpad. Running will cause joint strain at 96 kg and can burn away muscle instead of fat.

* When: Right after your gym training session, or first thing in the morning on an empty stomach.
* Duration: 25 to 30 minutes daily.
* Pace: A brisk, steady walking pace (around 4 to 5 km/h). This keeps you in the optimal "fat-burning zone" without impacting your muscle recovery. [8]

## 🏋️‍♂️ Beginner Gym Plan: Precise Form & Realistic Plate Loading
Note: Empty adjustable handles weigh approximately 2 kg, and the empty curl rod weighs approximately 5 kg. The plate assignments below reflect safe, highly realistic starting weights for a beginner at your height.
## 🔴 PUSH DAY (Monday & Thursday)

   1. Floor Dumbbell Press (Chest)
   * Starting Weight: 9.5 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put one 5kg plate and one 2.5kg plate on each dumbbell handle (5 + 2.5 + 2kg handle = 9.5kg).
      * Precise Form: Lie flat on your back on the floor. Hold the dumbbells directly over your chest. Lower your arms until your triceps gently touch the floor. Pause for a split second, then press back up. Do not bounce your elbows off the ground.
   2. Curl Rod Overhead Press (Shoulders)
   * Starting Weight: 15 kg total.
      * Plates: Put one 5kg plate on each side of the curl rod (5 + 5 + 5kg rod = 15kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand straight with feet shoulder-width apart. Rest the bar on your upper chest. Press the bar straight up over your head until your arms lock out. Keep your belly button sucked in tight so your lower back doesn't bend backward. [9] 
   3. Decline Push-ups (Lower Chest)
   * Starting Weight: Bodyweight.
      * Precise Form: Place your feet up on a bed or a sturdy chair, and put your hands flat on the floor in front of you. Lower your chest toward the floor, keeping your body in a straight line like a wooden plank, then push back up. [8] 
   4. Dumbbell Lateral Raises (Shoulder Width)
   * Starting Weight: 4 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 1kg plates on each dumbbell handle (1 + 1 + 2kg handle = 4kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand up straight. Hold dumbbells at your sides. Keeping a very slight bend in your elbows, raise your arms out to the sides until they are parallel to the floor. Lower them slowly. Do not swing your body to lift them.
   5. Overhead Tricep Extension (Triceps)
   * Starting Weight: 9.5 kg (You will only use one dumbbell handle).
      * Plates: Put one 5kg plate and one 2.5kg plate on a single dumbbell handle.
      * Precise Form: Sit down. Hold the dumbbell vertically with both hands cupping the underside of the top plate. Lift it over your head. Keep your elbows pointed forward (do not let them flare out) and lower the weight behind your neck, then extend your arms straight back up.
   6. Diamond Push-ups (Triceps & Inner Chest)
   * Starting Weight: Bodyweight (Perform these on your knees if normal ones are too difficult at first).
      * Precise Form: Get into a push-up position but place your index fingers and thumbs together so they form a diamond shape on the floor directly under your chest. Lower yourself down and push up. [2] 
   
## 🟢 PULL DAY (Tuesday & Friday)

   1. Curl Rod Bent-Over Rows (Upper Back)
   * Starting Weight: 20 kg total.
      * Plates: Put one 7.5kg plate on each side of the curl rod (7.5 + 7.5 + 5kg rod = 20kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand up, bend your knees slightly, and lean your torso forward from your hips until your back is almost parallel to the floor. Keep your back perfectly straight. Pull the bar up toward your belly button, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top.
   2. Single-Arm Dumbbell Row (Lats/Mid-Back)
   * Starting Weight: 12 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 5kg plates on a dumbbell handle (5 + 5 + 2kg handle = 12kg).
      * Precise Form: Place one knee and one hand on a flat bed or bench for support. Hold the dumbbell in your other hand, letting it hang straight down. Pull the dumbbell up by driving your elbow back toward your hip.
   3. Curl Rod Bicep Curls (Bicep Mass)
   * Starting Weight: 15 kg total.
      * Plates: Put one 5kg plate on each side of the curl rod (5 + 5 + 5kg rod = 15kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand straight. Hold the curl rod with an underhand grip (palms facing up). Pin your elbows against your ribcage. Curl the bar up toward your chin using only your biceps. Do not swing your upper body. [8] 
   4. Dumbbell Hammer Curls (Bicep Thickness)
   * Starting Weight: 7 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 2.5kg plates on each dumbbell handle (2.5 + 2.5 + 2kg handle = 7kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand tall holding dumbbells at your sides, but turn your hands so your palms face each other (like holding a hammer). Keep your elbows locked at your side and curl the weights up.
   5. Rear Delt Flyes (Posterior Shoulder)
   * Starting Weight: 4 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 1kg plates on each dumbbell handle (1 + 1 + 2kg handle = 4kg).
      * Precise Form: Bend completely forward at your hips until your chest is facing the floor. Let the dumbbells hang down. Raise your arms out straight to your sides like wings, focusing entirely on squeezing the back of your shoulders.
   6. Reverse Curl Rod Curls (Forearms)
   * Starting Weight: 10 kg total.
      * Plates: Put one 2.5kg plate on each side of the curl rod (2.5 + 2.5 + 5kg rod = 10kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand up and hold the curl rod with an overhand grip (palms facing down toward the floor). Curl the bar upward. You will feel an intense burn on the top of your forearms.
   
## 🔵 LEGS & CORE (Wednesday & Saturday)

   1. Dumbbell Goblet Squats (Quads & Thigh Shaping)
   * Starting Weight: 12 kg (Hold one dumbbell handle).
      * Plates: Put two 5kg plates on a single dumbbell handle.
      * Precise Form: Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulder-width. Hold the dumbbell vertically against your chest with both hands. Sit back and down as if sitting in a deep chair. Keep your chest up and push through your heels to stand back up.
   2. Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squats (Glutes & Single-Leg Strength)
   * Starting Weight: 7 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 2.5kg plates on each dumbbell handle (2.5 + 2.5 + 2kg handle = 7kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand about two feet in front of a chair or bed. Reach one foot back and rest the top of your foot on the chair. Lower your hips down until your back knee is close to the floor, then drive through your front foot to stand up. Complete all reps on one leg before switching.
   3. Curl Rod Romanian Deadlifts (Hamstrings & Lower Back)
   * Starting Weight: 20 kg total.
      * Plates: Put one 7.5kg plate on each side of the curl rod (7.5 + 7.5 + 5kg rod = 20kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand tall holding the bar in front of your thighs. Keeping your back perfectly straight and knees only slightly unlocked, push your hips straight backward. Lower the bar down your shins until you feel a deep stretch in the back of your thighs, then snap your hips forward to stand up.
   4. Dumbbell Calf Raises (Calves)
   * Starting Weight: 12 kg per dumbbell.
      * Plates: Put two 5kg plates on each dumbbell handle (5 + 5 + 2kg handle = 12kg).
      * Precise Form: Stand with the balls of your feet on the edge of a step or block so your heels hang off. Hold the dumbbells at your sides. Rise up onto your tiptoes as high as possible, hold for 1 second, then lower your heels below the level of the step to get a deep stretch.
   5. Weighted Floor Crunches (Upper Abs)
   * Starting Weight: Use one single 5 kg plate.
      * Precise Form: Lie flat on your back with knees bent. Hold the 5kg plate flat against your chest with both hands. Flex your abdominal muscles to raise your shoulders off the floor. Do not pull on your neck.
   6. Lying Leg Raises (Lower Abs & Lower Belly Fat)
   * Starting Weight: Bodyweight.
      * Precise Form: Lie flat on your back with legs straight out. Place your hands flat under your glutes to support your lower back. Keeping your legs straight, slowly lift them up until they form a 90-degree angle with the floor, then lower them slowly back down without letting your heels touch the ground. [8]
